Help

Everything you can do with Mabel, in one page.

What Mabel does

Mabel turns speech into text on your Mac, then pastes the result wherever your cursor is. Audio is recorded, transcribed, and deleted. Nothing is uploaded unless you explicitly choose the Groq cloud engine and supply your own API key.

Quick start

  1. Pick a microphone in Settings → General.
  2. Pick an engine in Settings → Engine. Local works offline; Groq cloud is faster but sends audio to Groq.
  3. If you chose Local, click Download to fetch the Whisper model (one-time, 466 MB or 1.5 GB).
  4. Place your cursor in any text field, press the hotkey, speak, release (or press again).
  5. Mabel pastes the transcription at your cursor.

The hotkey

Default is Cmd+D. Change it in Settings → General by clicking the hotkey field and pressing your new combination. Press Esc to cancel.

Toggle mode: press once to start, press again to stop and paste.
Push to talk mode: hold the hotkey while you speak, release to stop and paste.

AI cleanup (optional)

Settings → Engine → Cleanup. On builds with the local LLM runtime available, switch from Rules only to AI cleanup (local) to download Gemma 4 E4B (~5 GB, one-time) and run a local cleanup pass after Whisper. Runs fully on-device, never sends your audio or transcript anywhere.

Multilingual

Mabel auto-detects the language you're speaking. Whisper supports 100+ languages and transcribes in the source language (no auto-translation). If auto-detect picks the wrong language on a short utterance, just keep dictating — longer audio gives the detector more to work with.

Mabel on your desktop (companion)

Settings → System → Mabel on your desktop. Off by default. When on, Mabel walks across your screen now and then — she enters from one edge, pauses to sit and blink, then walks off. Adjust her size, how often she visits, and how long each visit lasts. Use Show now to preview, click again to dismiss mid-walk.

Live dictation

Live dictation is paused while the streaming worker is being stabilized. Current builds transcribe once when you stop recording, then paste a single clean result.

Local vs Groq cloud

Local uses Whisper running on your Mac. Audio never leaves the device. Slower than cloud, especially on the Medium model, but completely private.

Groq cloud uses Groq's hosted Whisper. Faster and more accurate on long audio, but each clip is sent to Groq's servers. Requires a free key from console.groq.com. Your key is stored in the macOS Keychain, never in a config file.

The overlay

A small floating panel appears at the top of your screen while you're recording, showing a live waveform. It floats over fullscreen apps and never steals focus, so the app you're typing into stays active. Drag it if you want to move it.

Permissions

  • Microphone — required to record. Grant when first prompted, or in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone.
  • Accessibility — required to paste at the cursor. Toggle Mabel on under System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility.
  • Automation (System Events) — required for the paste mechanism. Mabel asks for both Accessibility and Automation up front during the first-launch download, so you grant them once instead of being interrupted later.
  • Keychain — only if you save a Groq API key. Click Always Allow to avoid future prompts.

Privacy

  • Audio files are written to a temp directory, transcribed, then deleted.
  • Transcripts are never logged to disk.
  • No telemetry, no analytics, no phoning home.
  • Stats on the Home view (today, all-time, streak) are counts only, never content.
  • API keys live in the macOS Keychain.

Troubleshooting

Nothing happens when I press the hotkey. Check that the hotkey isn't already bound by another app. Try a different combination in Settings → General.

Recording starts but nothing pastes. Open System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility and confirm Mabel is toggled on. If you denied the Automation prompt, reset it with tccutil reset AppleEvents com.mabel.app in Terminal and try again.

Transcription is slow. Switch to the Small model in Settings → Engine, or try Groq cloud.

Wrong microphone. Pick the one you want in Settings → General.
